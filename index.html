History and Legislation

In 1892, every form of gambling was banned by the Canadian Criminal Code, however, things began to change less than ten years later. In 1900, raffles and bingo were permitted so long as they raised money for charitable causes, and another ten years after that, horse racing became legal. By 1925, gambling activities were permitted in fairs and exhibitions around the country.

The Criminal Code was amended again in 1969 to allow both the federal and provincial governments the chance to run lotteries to fund special projects. The first Canadian lottery was held in 1974 with the aim of raising money for the Montreal Olympic bid. Gradually through the 20th century, Canadian provinces and governments received more rights to run lotteries and horse racing events, plus open up new casinos replete with video slot machines. The legalization process became a trend and now casinos can be found in almost every province in the country.

Fast-forward to the 21st century, and the perception of gambling has shifted further, thrusting semi-professional and online gaming into the mainstream. Nowadays it’s not uncommon for people of all backgrounds to be found stacking up chips in poker halls and online sites.
